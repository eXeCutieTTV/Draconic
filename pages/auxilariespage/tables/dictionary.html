Auxiliary Definition Forms Notes
āhk do not! (prohibitive) defective - always in the second person
ciχād to have been able to, could (past abilitative) defective - often implies a counterfactual
ciχo to be able to, can (abilitative) defective - implies present tense
linæ to apprehensively, to timidly, to fear to (timitive mood) linād, luzinæ, luzinād
llēħ to begin to, to start (inchoative aspect) llad, N/A, N/A - past form often implies a counterfactual
llo to be going to, will (future tense) defective
llōd to have been going to, would (future in the past) defective - often implies a counterfactual
lūryχ to be in the middle of doing something (continuous aspect) lyrōd, N/A, N/A - in casual speech, the forms are typically realized as lurχ and lyrod - does not take prefix suffixes; see this.
mō to have done (perfect aspect); to finish doing (terminative aspect) mōrd, mōmo, mōmord - only the terminative aspect can be used in the gnomic
o to intentionally, to purposefully (intentional aspect) od, ō, ōd
oryħ (passive voice) oryħád, ōryħ, ōryħád - agent of the passive is marked with hu- "from"
q̇ōm to not be the middle of doing something (negative of continuous aspect) lūrxed, N/A, N/A - in casual speech, the forms are typically realized as q̇om and lūrxyd
rak do! (imperative) defective - always in the second person
tæ'hú to have to, must (obligative modality) tæ'ûd, tærhú, tær'ûd - past forms can sometimes imply a counterfactual (i.e. to be supposed to, but...)
thox to still be (continuative aspect) thoxad, thōx, thōxad
tolūr to be unable to (inabilitative aspect) defective
tolyr to almost, to nearly (defective aspect) defective
u to accidentally, to mistakenly (accidental aspect) ad, ū, ād
χar to hopefully, to wish to, to desire to (desiderative mood) χalód, χār, χalód
